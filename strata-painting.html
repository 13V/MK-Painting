Frequently Asked Questions — Strata Painters Adelaide

Everything body corporates and strata managers need to know

Do you need body corporate approval before painting common areas? +
Yes — painting of strata common areas, facades, and shared spaces typically requires a resolution from the body corporate or strata committee before work begins. We're familiar with this process and can provide all required documentation (quotes, product specifications, insurance certificates) to support your committee motion. Once approved, we schedule work to minimise disruption to residents.
How often should strata buildings be repainted in Adelaide? +
In Adelaide's climate, exterior strata painting typically needs refreshing every 7–10 years for masonry and rendered surfaces, and every 5–7 years for timber. Internal common areas generally need repainting every 5–8 years depending on traffic. A condition report from M&K can help your body corporate plan and budget accordingly.
How much does strata painting cost per unit in Adelaide? +
Strata painting costs vary significantly depending on the number of units, building height, surface condition, and scope of work. We provide free, detailed on-site quotes suitable for committee review, and can work with your strata manager to prepare a formal proposal. Call 0405 352 932 to book a site inspection.
Do you provide certificates of currency for strata painting work? +
Yes — we provide certificates of currency for our $20 million public liability insurance on request. This is standard for all strata and body corporate projects. We also carry builder's licence BLD251492 and can provide SWMS documentation for any site safety requirements.
